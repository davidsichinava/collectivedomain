News

Jan 2025

Event: Security and Defence in the South Caucasus

Partner David Sichinava spoke at a hybrid event on security and defence in the South Caucasus, held at the Ottawa Art Gallery on Friday, January 31st, 2025 (9:00 am–12:30 pm). The discussion focused on geopolitical issues in Armenia and Georgia. The event had limited in-person spaces.

Dec 2024

New Publication: Power and Protest — Georgians Fighting for their European Dream

Sahadeo, J., Sichinava, D., & Kotrikdaze, B. published a policy brief titled Power and Protest: Georgians Fighting for their European Dream through the Eastern European and Transatlantic Network (December 5, 2024).

Dec 2024

Workshop: The Impact of the War in Ukraine on the EU and its Eastern Policy

David Sichinava participated in an in-person workshop hosted by Carleton's Jean Monnet Centre of Excellence on December 5, 2024, examining the impact of the war in Ukraine on EU Eastern policy.

Sep 2024

New Publication: #SpendYourSummerInGeorgia

Harris-Brandts, S., Gogishvili, D., & Sichinava, D. published "#SpendYourSummerInGeorgia: Popular Geopolitics and Tourism Marketing in Georgia-Russian Relations" in Social and Cultural Geography (September 2024).

Oct 2024

Event: Elections in Georgia

David Sichinava presented at an event on October 30, 2024 examining elections in Georgia between Europe and Russia.

Nov 2023

Panel: Urban Influx in Tbilisi

Collective Domain partners participated in a panel discussion on urban influx and its effects on Tbilisi on November 16, 2023.

2021

SSHRC Insight Grant Awarded (2021–2026)

Suzanne Harris-Brandts (Principal Investigator) received a SSHRC Insight Grant of $152,000 for the project "Gardens Otherwise and Elsewhere: A Historical and Ethnographic Study of Georgian Gardens". Co-applicants include P. Manning (Trent University), A. Meneley (Trent), and T. Meneley (University of Toronto).

2020

Tbilisi Architecture Biennial 2020

Collective Domain participated in the Tbilisi Architecture Biennial 2020 with the workshop and exhibition "Build It and They Will Come — Bridging the Researcher-Practitioner Divide."

2020

Focus Groups in Batumi and Tskaltubo

Collective Domain conducted focus group research in Batumi and Tskaltubo as part of ongoing urban research in the South Caucasus region.

2019

Shota Rustaveli National Science Foundation Grant

Collective Domain received a Shota Rustaveli National Science Foundation Grant for urban research in Georgia.

2019

Green Mobilisations: Central Asia and South Caucasus

Collective Domain participated in the Green Mobilisations network, examining environmental activism across Central Asia and the South Caucasus.

2018

Infographics Database Launch

Collective Domain launched its public space infographics database — a Creative Commons resource documenting threats to public space in Tbilisi. The infographics are free to download, print, and share. View infographics →

2018

Website Launch

The Collective Domain website launched, presenting research on urban development, architecture, and public space in Tbilisi and the South Caucasus region.

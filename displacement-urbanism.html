Project Overview

Interconnections between Displacement and Urbanism

Over the past few decades, conflict-induced displacement has become both increasingly urban and protracted.

Our research examines how internally displaced persons (IDPs) navigate urban environments, how their presence reshapes cities, and how urban policy and design can better address the needs of displaced communities. We focus particularly on Georgia, Ukraine, and the wider post-Soviet space, where conflicts have produced large populations of long-term urban IDPs.

We examine the social and spatial insularity of IDP communities in collective centres, the hazards associated with displacement in Georgia, rapid response housing in Ukraine, and the broader political economy of displacement and urbanism.

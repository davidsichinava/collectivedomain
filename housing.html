Project Overview

Housing and Neighborhood Inclusion

Issues of housing and neighborhood inclusion are intimately connected to those of design.

Our research examines how housing policy, design, and urban development interact with questions of social inclusion, gentrification, and inequality in post-socialist cities. We focus particularly on the South Caucasus, where rapid urban transformation has produced significant housing inequalities.

Research areas include the transformation of Soviet mass-housing estates in Tbilisi, the conversion of residential courtyards, post-socialist gentrification and newbuild development, housing inequalities across Yerevan and Tbilisi, and rapid response housing for displaced persons in Ukraine.

Project Overview

Mapping the Erosion of Public Space in Tbilisi

Our research maps and documents the systematic erosion of shared public space across Tbilisi's diverse neighborhoods.

Through detailed spatial analysis of three distinct case study neighborhoods, we document the privatization, encroachment, and loss of courtyards, sidewalks, and other shared urban spaces. This research connects to our broader work on urban activism, housing, and spatial justice in post-socialist cities.

This project is supported by the SSHRC Insight Grant (2021–2026), with Suzanne Harris-Brandts as Principal Investigator.

Case Studies

Sololaki

Sololaki is one of Tbilisi's oldest neighborhoods, characterized by narrow winding streets and densely layered historical architecture. The neighborhood's distinctive urban fabric — courtyards, balconies, and shared passage ways — is under pressure from tourism development and private investment.

View Case Study Maps

Saburtalo

Saburtalo is a Soviet-era residential district in western Tbilisi, defined by mid-rise apartment blocks and the shared courtyards between them. Research documents how these communal courtyards are being converted to private parking, eroding one of the neighborhood's primary forms of shared public space.

View Case Study Maps

Marjanishvili

Marjanishvili, located on the north bank of the Mktvari River, is a mixed-use neighborhood undergoing rapid commercial transformation. Our research documents the encroachment of commercial uses on pedestrian sidewalks and the informal privatization of shared urban space.

View Case Study Maps

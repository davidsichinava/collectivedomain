Identity + Place Meanings

Architecture, Political Power, and the Production of Place

Project Overview

Architecture as Expression of Political Power

Architecture is a rich material expression of political power, national identity, and collective memory.

Our research examines how built environments encode, reinforce, and contest political ideologies and cultural identities across post-socialist cities. We trace how specific buildings and urban forms — from Soviet-era party headquarters to new mosques and cultural institutions — carry and transform political meanings over time.

A central case study is Tbilisi's Institute of Marx, Engels, and Lenin (IMELI) building, now repurposed as the Biltmore Hotel, which illustrates the layered political meanings embedded in post-socialist urban space. Research also examines how architectural heritage diplomacy shapes national identity in Georgia and neighboring states.

Heritage Diplomacy, Cultural Politics, and Tourism in the South Caucasus

Project Overview

Heritage and Tourism

Architecture is a rich material expression of political power and cultural identity. Our research examines how heritage and tourism intersect with geopolitics, diplomacy, and local communities.

This research focuses on concerns over the impacts of heritage tourism on local history representations, community life, and the built environment. We examine how heritage designation, cultural diplomacy, and tourism infrastructure reshape urban spaces and social relations across the South Caucasus region.

Key areas of inquiry include heritage diplomacy between Georgia and neighboring states, neo-Ottoman mosque construction in Adjara, Soviet-era resort towns and their displaced communities, and the role of architecture in Georgia's European aspirations.

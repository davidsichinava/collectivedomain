Project Overview

Special Economic Zones and Incentivized Urban Development

Economic zones are implemented by governments to diversify economies or permit exceptional economic practices in limited areas.

Our research examines how Special Economic Zones (SEZs) function as instruments of incentivized urban development, creating territories of exception within cities where standard planning regulations are suspended in favor of economic development goals. We focus particularly on Tbilisi and Baku, where SEZs have intersected with mega-event infrastructure and large-scale urban transformation.

A key finding of our research is how SEZs and mega-events often "coincide" as practices of exception — both deploying exceptional governance to accelerate urban transformation and bypass standard planning and community engagement processes.

Project Overview

Architecture, Urban Mobility, and Ideology in Tbilisi's Cable Cars

Tbilisi operates a distinctive global cable car system developed across Soviet and post-Soviet periods.

Construction occurred between 1953 and 1988 under Soviet governance, followed by decommissioning during the 1990s, with partial reactivation beginning in the 2010s to serve tourism demands.

Soviet-era cable cars originally connected residents to mountainous recreational areas while promoting communist ideology regarding leisure and collective mobility. From the 1960s onward, new lines facilitated worker transportation from residential microrayons to the city center, connecting to broader transit networks.

Contemporary cable car expansion prioritizes tourism over mass transit. The Rike Park to Narikala Fortress line and proposed Panorama project demonstrate market-driven urban development. This represents a fundamental shift away from the infrastructure's original function as accessible public transit toward selective tourist experiences.
